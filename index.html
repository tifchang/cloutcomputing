Understanding and forecasting Trump's Twitter performance

Introduction

How does Trump gain followers and so much engagement on Twitter? What specifically about his posts are engaging and have contributed to his dominant Twitter following? This analysis aims to uncover the truth and reveal tactics underlying his tweets. Through this analysis, I also hope to reveal patterns, similaries, and differences between Trump's tweets and other high performing users.

Data Sources

This analysis focuses on Trump but samples from other Top 10 Twitter accounts in order to compare and contrast Trump's account with other high performing accounts. I chose two types of accounts within and outside the same domain: pop culture figures and political figures. Using the twitteR packages, I access @katyperry, @KimKardashian, @BarackObama, and @realDonaldTrump's posts.

Lastly, to add a new dimension to Trump's model, I will be scraping Presidential Approval Ratings over time.

Do people gravitate towards positive tweets?

Looking into sentiment analysis across various users' tweets, I gained a broader understanding of the emotions users conveyed through their tweets. Intuitively, people would gravitate towards tweets that were either informative or made them feel good (positive/uplifting). But this analysis proves otherwise.

First, I analyzed pop culture icons like Katy Perry and Kim Kardashian and showed that they focus on promotional tweets, positive personal tweets, or uplifting feel-good tweets (cheesy one-liner life wisdom quotes). These tweets, especially the personal tweets, have performed highest.

Forecasting Trump's Tweets

Going into model building, I initially wanted to build a model that would predict the Presidential Approval Rating for Trump based off data extracted from his tweets, both observed and underlying statistics about the tweet (i.e., number of retweets, sentiment, etc.). However, given the constraints of the Twitter API, I was not able to pull tweets from a larger variety of weeks. Therefore, the Presidential Approval Rating (which is calculated by week) were the same for each week (and did not vary much from week to week) and were not as interesting to predict given the constraints. Therefore, I wanted to build a model that would predict a tweet's performance given specific keywords as well as other factors such as links and that week's approval rating. However, after trying various models, I could not emerge with a model that had strong predictive power.

I ran a linear and KNN model with Retweet Count as the dependent variable and a selection of keywords and other observed variables as independent variables. Variables were chosen to be in the model if they provided significant explanatory power. This was determined by the p values of the variables as coefficients in the regression. All models had poor RMSE values; the linear model had the lowest RMSE value at 7318.424, while KNN was 7471.77. The residual plots are shown below.

Errors across Models

Linear (Left) vs. KNN (Right)


While the linear model shows lower RMSE, the model seem to be always overshooting the true values. On the other hand, the KNN model shows variation around the true value as certain predictions are nearly spot on while others are drastically off in either direction. Unfortunately, the models were not accurate enough to predict retweets.

Predicting success in social systems remains an area still in exploration. Even more specifically, even with a substantial amount of data, it's still difficult to predict. There is a lot of uncertainty and variables that surround user engagement with Twitter posts. Logically, there is variation in the heterogeneous preferences and underlying reasons users engage with a post that is difficult to account for given the current models and variables.

Conclusion

Trump has a strong and controversial personality. However, this energy has gained significant attention on Twitter. On Google trends, Trump's popularity and internet interest is roughly 10x that of Katy Perry, Kim Kardashian and Obama. Though this analysis specifically dove into understanding Trump through text mining, it would be interesting going forward to be able to forecast his approval ratings or even specific stock prices as news sources have said that his tweets significantly impact company stock prices when he tweets about one. However, predicting social media performance remains an unanswered question and can also be further explored. Nonetheless, Trump's dynamic sentiments and controversial content keep our feeds interesting and never fails to engage, whether good or bad.
